# Research Design & Methodology

---

## 1. What is a Research Design?

A research design is a blueprint that guides a study. It addresses seven key elements:

1. **Purpose of the study** — what the research aims to achieve
2. **Type of research design** — the overall strategy chosen
3. **Nature & techniques of data collection** — how data will be gathered
4. **Research problem / hypotheses** — the core question being investigated
5. **Research method** — the approach used to conduct the study
6. **Data analysis methods** — how collected data will be processed
7. **Report preparation** — how findings will be presented

---

## 2. Types of Research Design

### 2.1 Exploratory Research

- Used when a problem **has not been clearly studied** before
- Goal: gather **preliminary information** to help define problems and formulate sound research questions
- Helps the researcher become familiar with phenomena before deeper investigation
- Must be **flexible** to allow exploration of different aspects of a problem

**Common techniques:**
- Literature review
- In-depth interviews
- Questionnaires
- Focus groups
- Case studies

**Example:** *Which cloud storage services are students interested in?*

---

### 2.2 Descriptive Research

- Aims to **describe** an event, situation, people, group, community, or phenomenon
- Focuses on **precise and systematic measurement** of dimensions
- Does **not** explain how, when, or why characteristics occurred

**Examples:**
- Distribution of a community population by age, sex, or education
- Estimating proportions of people with specific views or attitudes
- Case studies and preliminary observation of a group

---

### 2.3 Diagnostic Research

- Finds **relationships between causes** and suggests solutions
- Discovers and tests whether certain variables (factors) are **associated**
- Mostly motivated by hypotheses
- Seeks **immediate or timely solutions** to problems

---

### 2.4 Causal Research (Explanatory Research)

- Identifies the **extent and nature of cause-and-effect relationships**
- Assesses the **impact of specific changes** on existing norms or processes
- Focuses on analysis of relationships between variables

**Two main methods:**

| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Experimentation** | Conducted in a controlled setting (e.g., laboratory) |
| **Statistical Research** | Uses statistical tools to explore causal relationships |

**Example:** *A company investigates why fewer customers are using its cloud storage service — testing whether price is the cause of declining subscriptions.*

---

### Comparison of Research Design Types

| Feature | Causal | Exploratory | Descriptive |
|---|---|---|---|
| Uncertainty level | Clearly defined | Highly ambiguous | Partially defined |
| Key statement | Research hypotheses | Research question | Research question |
| When used | Later stages | Early stages | Later stages |
| Structure | Highly structured | Unstructured | Structured |

## 3. Qualitative vs. Quantitative Research

### Qualitative Research

- Explores **attitudes, behaviour, and experiences**
- Methods: interviews, focus groups, observations
- Seeks **in-depth opinions** from participants
- Involves **fewer participants** due to depth of data

### Quantitative Research

- Generates **statistics** through large-scale surveys
- Methods: questionnaires, structured interviews
- Data is extracted and analysed using **statistical methods**

> **Note:** Neither approach is better than the other — they are simply different and each has its own strengths and weaknesses.

---

## 4. Examples of Qualitative Research Methodologies

### 4.1 Action Research

- An **interactive inquiry process** that balances problem-solving with research
- Applied in the **social sciences**
- Researcher works **collaboratively** with participants — acting as a facilitator, not an outsider studying "on" people
- Popular in: organisational management, community development, education, and agriculture

**Four-stage cycle:**

```
Planning → Acting → Observing → Reflecting
```

This cycle may repeat multiple times until satisfactory change is achieved.

**Data collection tools used:** questionnaires, interviews, and focus groups.

---

### 4.2 Grounded Theory

- A **systematic methodology** introduced by **Glaser and Strauss (1967)**
- Applied mainly in **social science research**
- Involves constructing hypotheses and theories through **collecting and analysing data**
- Uses **inductive reasoning** — theory emerges *from* data rather than testing a pre-existing hypothesis

**Goal:** Develop a theory that explains the main concern of a population and how that concern is resolved.

**Data collection methods:** focus groups, interviews, and comprehensive literature review.

**Examples:**
- Online learners' main concern → finding time to study → theory: *temporal integration*
- Computer users' concern → GPU power → theory: *changing equipment*

---

## 5. Scientific Research

- Involves the **collection, organisation, and analysis** of information to increase understanding
- Some scientific research is classified under **quantitative research**
- Some fields (especially in computer science, mathematics, and engineering) do not require a traditional data collection process

**Examples of fields without traditional data collection:**
- Artificial Intelligence (AI)
- Cryptography
- Database Systems
- Computer Graphics

## 6. Developing a Research Plan

A research plan helps to:
- Organise ideas and identify **flaws or inadequacies**
- Provide an **inventory** of tasks and materials needed
- Serve as a document for **peer review and feedback**

### Key Components of a Research Plan

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Research Objective** | Clearly stated in 1–2 lines |
| **Problem Statement** | Explicitly stated so required information is clear |
| **Major Concepts** | Key variables to be measured, defined clearly |
| **Method** | Approach to be used in solving the problem |
| **Techniques** | Detailed techniques to be adopted |
| **Sampling & Data Processing** | Methods for sampling and handling data |
| **Pilot Test Results** | Results reported if a pilot test was conducted |
| **Time & Cost Budget** | Planned timeline and budget |
